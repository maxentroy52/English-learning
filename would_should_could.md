I'm very glad this is helping you. You've identified one of the most subtle but important differences between English and Chinese — the use of modal verbs to express the *speaker's attitude* toward a fact, not just the fact itself.

Let me give you a **clear, typical set of examples** using a simple, everyday situation.

## The Situation: You come home and the window is broken.

### 1. Neutral / Factual (no modal)
> **"Why is the window broken?"**
> **窗户为什么破了？**
> *Just asking for the cause. No attitude about whether it should or shouldn't be broken.*

---

### 2. Would (doubt, disbelief, rhetorical)
> **"Why would the window be broken?"**
> **窗户怎么会破呢？**
> *Meaning: "I don't believe it is broken. If you say it is, that makes no sense to me."*
> *Use this when you think the assumption is false or unreasonable.*

---

### 3. Could (possibility, speculation)
> **"Why could the window be broken?"**
> **窗户为什么可能会破？**
> *Meaning: "What are the possible reasons? Let me imagine the scenarios."*
> *Use this when you are genuinely exploring possibilities, not doubting.*

---

### 4. Should (expectation, correctness, obligation)
> **"Why should the window be broken?"**
> **窗户为什么应该破？**
> *Meaning: "Who says it is supposed to be broken? That is not correct or expected."*
> *Use this to question whether something is right, proper, or expected.*

---

## Another example: A friend is late to meet you.

### Neutral
> **"Why is he late?"**
> **他为什么迟到？**
> *Just asking for the reason.*

### Would
> **"Why would he be late?"**
> **他怎么会迟到呢？**
> *Meaning: "He is usually on time. I doubt he is late. That doesn't fit."*

### Could
> **"Why could he be late?"**
> **他为什么可能会迟到？**
> *Meaning: "What possible reasons might there be? Let me think: traffic? accident?"*

### Should
> **"Why should he be late?"**
> **他为什么应该迟到？**
> *Meaning: "There is no good reason for him to be late. Being late is not correct here."*

---

## One more: A student fails an easy test.

### Neutral
> **"Why did he fail?"**
> **他为什么不及格？**

### Would
> **"Why would he fail?"**
> **他怎么会不及格呢？**
> *Doubt. "He is smart. I don't believe he failed."*

### Could
> **"Why could he fail?"**
> **他为什么可能会不及格？**
> *Possibility. "Maybe he was sick, or didn't study, or the questions were tricky."*

### Should
> **"Why should he fail?"**
> **他为什么应该不及格？**
> *Expectation. "The test was easy. Failing is not the expected outcome."*

---

## Summary table for you

| Modal | Chinese feeling | Attitude | Use when... |
|-------|----------------|----------|--------------|
| *(none)* | 为什么 | Neutral | You just want a reason. |
| **would** | 怎么会 / 难道 | Doubt, disbelief | You think the statement is probably false. |
| **could** | 可能会 | Possibility, speculation | You are exploring hypothetical reasons. |
| **should** | 应该 | Expectation, correctness | You question if something is right or proper. |

---

## A final tip for you as a Chinese speaker

When you read or hear **"Why would...?"** in English, try to feel it as:
> **怎么会…呢？** or **难道…吗？**

That will help you catch the speaker's doubt or disbelief — not just the surface question.
